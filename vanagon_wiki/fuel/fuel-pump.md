## Overview

The WBX fuel pump is an electric pump mounted in or near the fuel tank
at the front of the van. Failure causes hard starting, stalling, or
complete no-start conditions.

## Symptoms of Failing Fuel Pump

- Hard to start when hot (vapor lock or weak pump)
- Stalls at highway speeds under load
- No start — no fuel pressure
- Intermittent stalling

## Quick Test

Turn ignition to position II (key on, engine off). You should hear
the fuel pump prime for 2-3 seconds. If no sound, check the fuel
pump relay first before condemning the pump.

## Fuel Pump Relay

The fuel pump relay is the first thing to check on a no-start.
A failed relay kills the pump without any other symptoms.
Standard VW relay — inexpensive. Always carry a spare.

## Hot Start Issues

A common WBX complaint is hard starting when the engine is hot.
Causes include:
- Weak fuel pump not maintaining pressure
- Vapor in the fuel lines (heat soak)
- Failed check valve in the pump (pressure drops when hot)

Some owners install a return line upgrade to keep fuel circulating
and prevent vapor lock. TheSamba has detailed threads on this.

## Ford/NAPA Fuel Pump Alternative

A commonly discussed alternative is a Ford or NAPA fuel pump that
fits with minor modification. Less expensive than OEM VW pump.
TheSamba FAQ has a specific thread on this with part numbers.
