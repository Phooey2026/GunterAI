## Thermostat Housing — Important Note

On 2WD models 1986-1991, the thermostat housing is **plastic**.
On Syncro (4WD) models, it is **aluminum**.

The plastic housing is a known weak point — it can crack, especially
if overtightened or if the engine has been overheated. Van Cafe and
GoWesty sell a conversion kit to upgrade to the aluminum Syncro housing.
This upgrade is recommended, especially on high-mileage vans.

**Van Cafe aluminum housing kit:** 025-121-115-ALUMKITA
Includes housing, fuel injection temp sensor, gauge temp sensor,
thermostat seal, and all hardware.

## Replacement Procedure

The thermostat is located at the rear of the engine, accessible from
the engine hatch. It should be replaced:
- Every 2 years with coolant flush
- Any time the cooling system is opened
- If the van has overheated

**Failure modes:**
- Fails CLOSED — engine overheats (more dangerous)
- Fails OPEN — engine runs cold, poor fuel economy, heater issues

## 80°C vs 87°C Thermostat

Some owners install a cooler 80°C thermostat hoping to prevent overheating.
GoWesty does NOT recommend this. The engine needs to reach proper operating
temperature for complete combustion, oil viscosity, and to prevent
condensation in the oil. Stick with the 87°C spec thermostat.
