## Overview

The main water pump is engine-driven and circulates coolant when the engine
is running. It is distinct from the auxiliary electric water pump — see
[[cooling/aux-water-pump]] for that critical component.

## Replacement Interval

Replace at 60,000-80,000 miles proactively, or any time the cooling
system is opened for major work. Water pump failure causes immediate
overheating and can result in engine damage.

**Signs of failing water pump:**
- Coolant leak from the pump weep hole (small hole on pump body)
- Grinding noise from the pump bearing
- Overheating without other obvious cause

## Procedure Notes

The water pump is located at the rear of the engine, accessible from
the engine hatch. The procedure involves:
1. Draining the cooling system
2. Removing the drive belt
3. Removing the pump
4. Installing new pump with new gasket
5. Refilling and bleeding the cooling system

Always replace the gasket when replacing the pump. Apply a thin bead
of sealant to the gasket per Bentley spec.

## Timing with Other Services

If you are doing a head gasket job or any major cooling system work,
replace the water pump at the same time. The incremental labor cost
is minimal compared to opening the system again later.
