## Overview

The 2WD Vanagon rear wheel bearings are sealed units pressed into the
rear hub. Unlike the front bearings they are not adjustable or
serviceable — when worn, replace.

## Part Numbers — 2WD (1980-1991)

## Symptoms of Worn Rear Bearings

- Grinding or rumbling noise from rear, changes with speed
- Noise does NOT change when turning (distinguishes from front bearings)
- Looseness when rocking the wheel

## Procedure Notes

Rear bearing replacement requires pressing the old bearing out and
the new one in. A hydraulic press is recommended. The bearing must
be pressed in squarely to avoid damage.

## Rear Bearing vs Front Bearing Diagnosis

To determine which end has the bad bearing:
- Worn front bearing: noise changes when turning left or right
- Worn rear bearing: noise is constant regardless of steering input
- Jack up one wheel at a time and spin by hand to isolate the side
