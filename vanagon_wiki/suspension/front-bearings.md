## Overview

The 1984-1991 2WD Vanagon uses tapered roller bearings in the front hubs.
They are serviceable — they can be repacked with grease and adjusted for
preload. The inner and outer bearings are different sizes.

**Important:** These are NOT the same as the sliding door roller bearings.
See [[body/sliding-door]] for door roller bearings.

## Procedure Notes (Bentley p.1070)

1. Pressing out the hub **destroys the bearing** — it cannot be reused
2. Pack BOTH bearings thoroughly with high-temperature wheel bearing grease
3. Torque adjusting nut to **7 ft-lb (10 Nm)** while rotating hub
4. Back off nut until thrust washer can just be moved with a screwdriver tip
5. Secure with a **new cotter pin**
6. Replace grease seal every time — never reuse

## Symptoms of Worn Front Bearings

- Grinding or rumbling noise that changes with speed
- Noise changes when turning left vs right (indicates which side)
- Looseness or play when rocking the wheel top-to-bottom
- At 202,000 miles: proactive inspection strongly recommended

## Service Interval

No fixed mileage interval — inspect at every brake service.
Repack with grease every 50,000 miles if not replacing.
At high mileage (150K+) replacement rather than repacking is advisable.
