## CRITICAL DISTINCTION — Read Before Proceeding

**2WD Vanagon:** Has CV joints on the REAR axle ONLY.
The front wheels on a 2WD Vanagon use tapered roller bearings — there are
NO CV joints at the front on a 2WD vehicle.

**Syncro (4WD) Vanagon:** Has CV joints at ALL FOUR wheels — both the
front and rear axles use CV joints as part of the 4WD drivetrain.

**Bentley pages 1070-1079 cover Syncro front axle CV joints ONLY.**
These pages DO NOT apply to 2WD Vanagons.

When a user has a 2WD Vanagon asking about CV joints:
- The symptom is at the REAR axle only
- Refer to Bentley p.1036-1042 for the 2WD rear axle
- Do NOT recommend front CV joint work on a 2WD vehicle

---

## 2WD Vanagon — Rear Axle and CV Joint Design

The 2WD Vanagon rear axle consists of two drive axle shafts (one left,
one right). Each axle shaft has a CV joint bolted at BOTH ends:

- **Inner CV joint:** Bolts to the transmission output flange with 6 bolts
- **Outer CV joint:** Bolts to the rear wheel hub flange with 6 bolts

The axle shaft itself remains in the vehicle during CV joint service.
The CV joints unbolt from each end of the shaft — this is NOT a
half-shaft design where the shaft is removed. No pressing is required.
No special tools are needed beyond basic hand tools.

This is a simple, accessible repair that does not require lifting the
engine or removing the hub assembly.

---

## Symptoms of Worn or Failed Rear CV Joints (2WD)

- Clicking or popping noise from the REAR when turning under power
- Clunking from the rear under acceleration or deceleration
- Grease splattered on the inside face of the rear wheels (torn boot)
- Vibration at highway speed (worn joint)
- Grease on the undercarriage near the rear axle

---

## CV Boot vs Full Joint Replacement

**Boot replacement only:** If the boot is torn but the joint is not yet
clicking or rough, regrease and rebook the existing joint. This is a
$30-50 parts job and straightforward.

**Full CV joint replacement:** If the joint is clicking, clunking, or
feels rough when rotated by hand, replace the entire CV joint. The joint
unbolts from the axle flange — no pressing required.

**Complete axle replacement:** GoWesty and other suppliers offer complete
pre-built rear axle assemblies. This is the easiest option if both joints
on one side need replacement.

## 2WD Rear CV Joint Procedure (Bentley p.1036-1042)

### Removal
1. Raise and support the rear of the vehicle safely
2. Remove the rear wheel
3. Remove the 6 bolts securing the OUTER CV joint to the rear hub flange
4. Remove the 6 bolts securing the INNER CV joint to the transmission flange
5. Remove the axle assembly (shaft + both CV joints) from the vehicle
6. The CV joints unbolt from the axle shaft — no pressing required

### Inspection
- Rotate each CV joint by hand — it should feel smooth with no roughness
- Inspect boots for cracks, tears, or hardening
- Check for grease loss — a joint that has run dry needs replacement

### Boot Replacement
- Clean the joint thoroughly
- Replace all boot clamps with new ones — never reuse
- Pack with the correct grease — see grease specification below
- Install new boot and equalize pressure before clamping

### Reinstallation
- Use new CV bolts and serrated lock washers — never reuse hardware
- Torque inner CV bolts to transmission flange: 32 ft-lb (Bentley p.1037)
- Torque outer CV bolts to hub flange: 32 ft-lb (Bentley p.1037)

## Grease Specification

Use only CV joint grease included with the joint or boot kit (typically
G6.3 or equivalent Molykote grease). Do not substitute wheel bearing
grease, lithium grease, or other types. The correct grease is critical
for CV joint longevity.

GoWesty note: Most installers omit the small-end boot clamp on rear
joints — grease will not fling out of an unclamped small end. The
large-end clamp is required.

## Syncro (4WD) — Front CV Joints

Syncro models have CV joints at the front axle as part of the front
differential and front driveshaft assembly. This is a completely
different system from the 2WD rear axle.

Bentley pages 1070-1079 cover Syncro front CV joint service only.
The ball-hub/cage/ball disassembly described on those pages,
the 400kg clamp tool, and the pressing procedures
DO NOT apply to 2WD Vanagon rear CV joints.
