## Tire Size

**Standard size:** 185R14C

The "C" designation is critical — it denotes a commercial/van-rated tire
with higher load capacity than a standard passenger tire. Do not substitute
a non-C rated tire.

## Tire Pressure — Westfalia Camper Models (1980-1991 2WD)

| Position | Pressure | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Front | **39 psi** | Check cold |
| Rear | **48 psi** | Check cold — significantly higher than front |

**Always check when cold** — tires sitting for 3+ hours or driven less than 1 mile.
Never reduce pressure from warm tires.

### Why the Large Front/Rear Difference?

The Vanagon has extreme rear weight bias:
- Engine, transmission, and fuel tank are all at the rear
- Full Westfalia kit (refrigerator, stove, sink, water tank, pop-top) adds more rear weight
- Front carries only passengers, spare tire, and (when full) fuel tank

The higher rear pressure supports this weight and maintains proper tire contact
patch geometry under load and cornering. Running equal pressures would result
in overloaded rear tires — a blowout risk at highway speeds, especially in
summer heat at elevation (Eastern Sierra conditions).

**Note on fuel tank:** The gas tank is actually at the front, which provides
some counterbalance. However at typical running levels (1/4 tank) this adds
only ~24 lbs — negligible vs the rear weight.

## Tin Top (Non-Westfalia) Models

GL passenger van, Transporter, and other non-camper models carry less
rear weight. Their tire pressure specifications differ from the Westfalia.
**Always check the doorjamb sticker** for your specific model.
The Bentley references this sticker at p.1326 as the authoritative source.

## Syncro (4WD) Models

Syncro models have different tire pressure requirements due to different
weight distribution and the transfer case. Consult your doorjamb sticker.

## Recommended Tire Brands — 185R14C

Major European manufacturers (Michelin, Continental) discontinued this size
years ago. Current best options:

| Brand | Model | Notes |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Hankook | Vantra LT RA18 | Top of the line for Vanagons, ~$150-180 each |
| Cosmo | El Jefe | Excellent value, Luna's current tire — owner confirmed |
| Sailun | Commercio VX1 | Good Thailand-manufactured alternative |
| Westlake | CR960 | Budget option, acceptable quality |

**Owner note (Jay/Luna):** Cosmo El Jefe is a great tire for the price —
much cheaper than Hankook Vantra but performs well. Found on eBay.
Thailand-manufactured alternatives have improved significantly in recent years.

## Break-In Period

New tires do not have maximum traction for the first 100-200 miles.
Drive at moderate speeds during break-in — avoid aggressive maneuvers.

## Spare Tire

The spare is stored under the front hood. Include it in pressure checks.
A spare at correct pressure is critical on the Vanagon — the rear
weight distribution makes a rear flat particularly dangerous.
