## Overview

All 1980-1991 Vanagons use front disc brakes. The front brakes do the
majority of braking work given the rear-heavy weight distribution.

## Pad Wear Inspection (Bentley p.1018)

Minimum pad thickness: **2mm**

The Bentley notes that if you cannot insert a gauge between the spreader
spring and the backing plate, the pads are at the 2mm limit and must
be replaced immediately.

## Rotor Inspection

Measure rotor thickness with a micrometer at multiple points around
the circumference. Minimum thickness is stamped on the rotor hat.
Discard any rotor showing deep grooves, cracks, or blue heat discoloration.

## Procedure Notes (Bentley p.1021)

1. Before opening the caliper, siphon some fluid from the master cylinder
   reservoir — pushing the piston back will force fluid up the line
2. Use a C-clamp or brake piston tool to push the caliper piston back
3. Remove caliper — support it with wire, do not let it hang from the hose
4. Remove pads noting orientation and any shims
5. If reusing pads, mark them so inner/outer are not swapped
6. Install new pads and lubricate sliding surfaces with brake grease
7. Use **new self-locking lower caliper bolt** — torque to 35 Nm / 26 ft-lb
8. After installation, pump brake pedal firmly several times to seat pads
   before moving the vehicle

## Bedding New Pads

After installation, perform several moderate stops from 30 mph to bed
the pads to the rotors. Avoid hard stops for the first 200 miles.
