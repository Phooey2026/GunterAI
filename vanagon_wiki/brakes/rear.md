## Overview

Rear brake configuration changed mid-production:
- **1980-1985:** Rear drum brakes
- **1986-1991:** Rear disc brakes (same era as Digifant upgrade)

The rear disc calipers also incorporate the parking brake mechanism,
which uses a separate set of shoes inside the rotor hat. This makes
rear caliper service more complex than the front.

| Part | VW Part Number | Notes |

Drum brake service requires adjusting the shoes after replacement
for correct drum clearance.

## Parking Brake

The parking brake operates on the rear wheels. On 1986-1991 models
with rear disc brakes, the parking brake uses drum-in-hat shoes
inside the rear rotor. These shoes require periodic adjustment and
eventual replacement.

A poorly adjusted or seized parking brake cable is a common issue
on older Vanagons. Lubricate cable guides and check adjustment
at every service.

## Brake Balance

Given the extreme rear weight bias of the Vanagon, brake balance
is important. The proportioning valve ensures the rear brakes
don't lock before the fronts. Do not modify the brake system
in ways that upset this balance.
