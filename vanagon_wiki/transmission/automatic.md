## Overview

The 1980-1991 Vanagon automatic is a 3-speed torque converter automatic.
It is generally reliable when properly maintained. The most common
failure mode is neglected fluid changes leading to clutch pack wear.

## Fluid Specification

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Fluid type | ATF Dexron II or Dexron III |
| Total capacity | Approx 5.5 quarts |
| Service fill (drain and fill) | Approx 3.0 quarts |
| Change interval | **30,000 miles** |

**Note:** A full flush requires multiple drain/fill cycles to replace
all the fluid, as the torque converter retains fluid. A simple drain
and fill replaces about half the fluid — still worthwhile.

## Kickdown Adjustment

The kickdown cable connects the throttle to the transmission and triggers
downshifts when the throttle is fully depressed. Incorrect adjustment
causes:
- Shift points too early or too late
- Transmission hunting between gears
- Poor fuel economy

The kickdown cable adjustment is covered in detail in the Bentley and
is a common adjustment needed after throttle work or cable replacement.
GoWesty has a technical article on kickdown adjustment.

## Common Problems

| Symptom | Likely Cause |
|---------|-------------|
| Slipping in 1st or 2nd | Worn clutch packs — fluid neglect |
| Harsh shifts | Low fluid or incorrect fluid type |
| Won't shift into 3rd | Kickdown cable adjustment |
| Delayed engagement | Low fluid level |
| Fluid dark brown/burnt | Overdue service — change immediately |

## Transmission Service

Luna's transmission was serviced in September 2025 at 200,457 miles.

The drain plug is on the bottom of the transmission pan. There is no
easily accessible filter on most service procedures — the pan must
be dropped for a full service. Many owners do drain-and-fill services
without dropping the pan.

## GoWesty Warning

GoWesty has a detailed article on automatic transmission failure prevention.
Key points: use only Dexron II/III, change every 30,000 miles, and never
let the fluid run dark. Transmission rebuilds are expensive.
