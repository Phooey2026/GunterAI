## Overview

The manual transmission Vanagon uses a 4-speed (early) or 5-speed (late)
gearbox. Generally more reliable than the automatic when properly maintained.

## Fluid Specification

**CRITICAL:** Use GL-4 gear oil ONLY. **Never use GL-5.**

GL-5 gear oil contains sulfur compounds that attack the yellow metal
(brass) synchronizers in VW transmissions. Using GL-5 will destroy
the synchros over time.

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Fluid type | GL-4, 75W-90 |
| Capacity | Approx 2.5 quarts |
| Change interval | 30,000 miles |

Redline MTL is a popular choice among Vanagon owners — it meets GL-4
spec and improves shift quality.

## Common Problems

| Symptom | Likely Cause |
|---------|-------------|
| Grinding when shifting | Worn synchros, often from GL-5 use |
| Hard to get into gear | Clutch adjustment or hydraulic issue |
| Pops out of gear | Worn shift forks or detent |

## Clutch

The manual transmission Vanagon clutch is hydraulically actuated.
The clutch master and slave cylinders should be inspected for leaks
at every service. Clutch fluid is shared with the brake system on
some models — check Bentley for your specific year.

## Shift Linkage

The 1980-1982 Vanagon shift linkage is notably poor — long, vague,
and difficult to use. GoWesty has a specific article on why the early
system is problematic. The 1983+ linkage is significantly improved.
