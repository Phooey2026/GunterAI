## Oil Specifications — 2.1L Wasserboxer

| Spec | Value |
|------|-------|
| Capacity (with filter) | 4.0 quarts |
| Recommended viscosity | 20W-50 conventional |
| Change interval | 3,000 miles |

**On synthetic oil:** GoWesty and most WBX specialists recommend conventional oil,
not synthetic, for high-mileage engines. The WBX has rope-style rear main seals
and older gasket materials that may weep with the lower viscosity of synthetics.
Some owners run 15W-40 diesel oil successfully.

## Oil Filter

See [[parts/cross-reference]] for full part numbers.

**Recommended filters:**
- **Mann W719/12** — OEM-quality, specifically prevents "Death Buzzer" syndrome
- **WIX 51342** — Preferred US option, widely available, quality anti-drainback valve
- **Mahle OC 108** — OEM supplier, equivalent to Mann

**Death Buzzer syndrome:** Some off-brand filters have inadequate anti-drainback
valves. After sitting overnight, oil drains back from the filter. On startup,
there is a momentary low oil pressure condition that triggers the oil pressure
buzzer. Using a quality filter with a good anti-drainback valve eliminates this.

## Oil Pressure Specifications

| Condition | Minimum Pressure |
|-----------|-----------------|
| At idle (warm) | 0.3 bar / 4.4 psi |
| At 2000 rpm (warm) | 1.8 bar / 26 psi |

## Oil Drain Plug
- VW part number: N-011-916-4
- Thread: M14 x 1.5
- Torque: 25 Nm / 18 ft-lb
- Always use a new copper crush washer

## Oil Change Procedure Notes
The WBX drain plug is accessed from below. The oil filter is also underneath.
On Westfalia models, the engine hatch in the floor must be opened.
Expect to spill some oil — have rags ready. The filter location makes
a clean change difficult without an oil filter removal tool.
