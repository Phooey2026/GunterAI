## Overview

Head gasket failure is the most common catastrophic failure on the Wasserboxer engine.
It is almost always preventable. The root cause is usually overheating, which is almost
always caused by auxiliary water pump failure.

## Symptoms of Head Gasket Failure

- White smoke from exhaust (coolant burning in cylinders)
- Coolant loss with no visible external leak
- Milky oil on dipstick or oil cap (coolant mixing with oil)
- Overheating with no obvious cause
- Bubbles in coolant reservoir
- Sweet smell from exhaust
- Engine misfires after warmup

**Important:** Many WBX head gasket failures occur without the temperature gauge
ever showing overheating. The localized boiling happens in the heads before the
coolant temp sensor detects it. See [[cooling/temperature-sensors]]

## Prevention — The Most Important Section

1. **Test the auxiliary water pump at every service.** Turn key to position II
   (accessories on, engine off). You should hear the pump running. If silent,
   replace immediately. See [[cooling/aux-water-pump]]

2. **Flush coolant every 2 years.** Old coolant loses its anti-corrosion properties
   and accelerates aluminum head corrosion.

3. **Never ignore the temperature gauge.** Any excursion above normal is serious
   on a WBX. Pull over immediately.

4. **Replace thermostat at cooling system services.** They fail closed (overheating)
   and are cheap insurance. See [[cooling/thermostat]]

## Diagnosis

### Compression test
Normal compression: 145-175 psi per cylinder
Failed head gasket: one or two cylinders significantly lower, or coolant in cylinder

### Leak-down test
More accurate than compression test. More than 10% leakdown indicates a problem.

### Block test (combustion leak test)
Chemical test of coolant for combustion gases. Most definitive non-invasive test.
Available at auto parts stores as a kit.

## Replacement Notes

- The WBX has two separate cylinder heads (one per side, 2 cylinders each)
- Each head has its own gasket
- GoWesty sells improved head gaskets — worth the premium over stock
- Always replace head bolts — they are torque-to-yield and cannot be reused
- Torque sequence and values are critical — follow Bentley exactly
- Machine the head surface if any warpage is detected

## Cost Reality

A head gasket job on a WBX is expensive at a shop due to access difficulty.
Many owners choose to do a full rebuild at this point rather than just the gasket.
GoWesty's rebuilt heads are worth considering — they address the known weak points
in the stock casting.
