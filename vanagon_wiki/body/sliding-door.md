## Overview

The Vanagon sliding door rides on rollers (guide rollers) that run in
a track. These rollers wear over time, causing the door to sag, bind,
or become difficult to open and close.

## IMPORTANT: These Are NOT Wheel Bearings

The sliding door guide rollers are a completely different component from
the front wheel bearings. Do not confuse them. See [[suspension/front-bearings]]
for wheel bearing information.

The guide rollers are small specialty bearings specific to the sliding
door track system. They are NOT standard skateboard bearings despite
what some TheSamba forum threads suggest.

## Symptoms of Worn Rollers

- Sliding door drops at the rear when opening
- Door requires lifting to latch
- Grinding or clicking when opening/closing
- Door difficult to open or close smoothly

## Adjustment

Before replacing rollers, try adjusting the door. The Vanagon sliding
door has multiple adjustment points:
- Upper track roller height
- Lower guide block fore/aft
- Latch striker plate position

A properly adjusted door should open and close smoothly with one hand
and latch without slamming.

## Roller Replacement

Roller replacement requires removing the door or at minimum removing
the roller assembly from the track. The procedure is in the Bentley.
Access is tight but possible without removing the door completely.
