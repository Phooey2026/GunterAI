## Overview

The Westfalia camper conversion was factory-installed by Westfalia
Fahrzeugwerke in Germany. It transforms the Vanagon into a fully
self-contained camper with kitchen, sleeping, and storage facilities.

## Camper Equipment — 1987 Westfalia

## Pop-Top

The pop-top canvas seal is a common maintenance item. The original canvas
deteriorates with UV exposure and age. Replacement canvas is available
from several suppliers (Van Cafe, Bus Depot, GoWesty).

**Pop-top latch:** The latch mechanism can loosen over time causing the
top to rattle or not seal properly. Check and adjust periodically.

## Dometic Refrigerator (3-Way)

The Dometic refrigerator runs on:
- **12V DC** — from vehicle battery (poor efficiency, for travel only)
- **120V AC** — when connected to shore power (most efficient)
- **Propane** — for camping without hookups

**Common issues:**
- Cooling fins clogged with dust/debris — clean annually from the outside vent
- Burner needs cleaning if propane mode isn't lighting
- 12V mode not cooling — normal, 12V mode is weak

## Propane System

The propane tank is typically mounted on the outside rear of the van.
Inspect hoses and connections annually for cracks or leaks.
Use soapy water to check for leaks at connections.

## Auxiliary Battery (House Battery)

The Westfalia typically has a separate house battery under one of the
seats or in a storage compartment. This powers the refrigerator, lights,
and water pump when not running the engine.

See [[electrical/charging]] for house battery charging information.

## Rear Coolant Heater

The rear heater uses engine coolant to heat the living space. It only
works when the engine is warm. Some owners replace it with a propane
Propex heater for camping when the engine isn't running.

**If rear heater is not working:** Check the coolant valve, heater
core for blockage, and blower motor.

## Water System

Fresh water tank capacity: approximately 7 gallons
The manual pump is preferred over electric — simpler and more reliable.
Sanitize the water system with a dilute bleach solution seasonally.
